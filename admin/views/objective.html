Tool Objective · Hard Requirements

AI should make us faster —
not make us clean up its mess

A lot of people vibe-code AI slop and ship it. That is not what this is. This tool exists to give one person the output of 50 — without sacrificing the quality, taste, or voice that makes BBB worth following in the first place.

What this tool IS
An engine, not a creator
AI handles the heavy lifting — research, structure, first drafts, formatting, platform adaptation. Elaina provides the creative vision, the real assets, and the final word.
A friction reducer
The parts that used to take days — writing 6 platform-adapted captions, scripting 3 reels, building carousel copy — should take minutes. Elaina's job is direction and approval, not execution from scratch.
A multiplier of one person
What previously required a content strategist, copywriter, designer, scheduler, and social media manager — this tool enables Elaina to do solo, at a high quality standard, consistently.
A system that learns
Every approval, edit, and rejection trains the system. The longer it runs, the less Elaina has to correct — because the AI gets closer to what she would have written herself.
What this tool is NOT
An autopilot
Nothing publishes automatically. Nothing goes live without Elaina's eyes on it. The approval step is not optional — it's the whole point.
A visual generator
AI does not generate the visuals. Elaina supplies the photos, videos, and assets — real moments from real events. AI helps design and arrange them in Canva, not replace them with stock or generated imagery.
A slop machine
Generic AI content that sounds like every other brand is a failure state, not a starting point. If the output isn't close to publish-ready, the system needs fixing — not Elaina's time.
A substitute for taste
Elaina's editorial judgment is the product. AI executes it. The moment this tool starts pushing Elaina toward what's easier to generate rather than what's right for BBB, it has failed.
Non-negotiable

Hard requirements

Must all be true
01
Elaina's voice is always the voice
Every draft must sound like Elaina wrote it. If it reads like AI, it goes back. AI adapts to her — she never adapts her voice to fit AI output.
Hard rule
02
All visuals are supplied by Elaina
No AI-generated imagery. No stock photos. All photos, videos, and assets come from real BBB events and moments. The AI's job is to arrange and design — not to fabricate.
Hard rule
03
Every piece of content is grounded in something real
A session, a book, a member story, a real conversation. Nothing is invented for the sake of content. Generic topics that could apply to any brand do not exist here.
Hard rule
04
Drafts must be close to publish-ready
Minor tweaks, yes. Rewrites from scratch, no. If Elaina is spending more time fixing AI output than she would have writing it herself, the system has failed and needs to be improved — not worked around.
Hard rule
05
Nothing goes live without Elaina's approval
Every single piece. No exceptions. No scheduled auto-publish without a manual sign-off first. The approval step is the quality gate — removing it breaks the whole model.
Hard rule
06
The tool must save time, not create new work
If any part of this pipeline adds friction, confusion, or cleanup time for Elaina, that part gets rebuilt. The benchmark: is this faster and better than doing it manually?
Hard rule
07
Quality is the metric — not volume
Hitting 6 posts a week with 6 mediocre pieces is a worse outcome than 4 excellent ones. The schedule is a target, not a mandate. Good content ships. Content that isn't ready doesn't.
Principle
08
Feedback flows back into the system
Every edit Elaina makes, every rejection, every "this doesn't sound like me" — feeds back to improve the next output. The system gets tighter over time, not looser.
Principle
"
Before anything ships, ask one question: would Elaina have written this herself, and would she be proud to put her name on it?
If the answer is no — it doesn't go out.
